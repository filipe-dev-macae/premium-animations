# Micro Interactions

Micro-interactions make interfaces feel responsive. They belong on controls, feedback, toggles, copy buttons, menus, accordions, forms, modals, drawers, tabs, and dashboard updates.

## Rules

- Feedback should be immediate.
- Keep movement small.
- Preserve focus states.
- Provide keyboard equivalents.
- Do not rely on hover for essential content.
- Avoid routine celebration.
- Keep errors calm and useful.
- Preserve layout dimensions during loading.

## Examples

- Copy button changes icon and label briefly.
- Toggle thumb moves with a short spring.
- Accordion icon rotates while content opens.
- Form field validates after blur or submit.
- Dashboard value update crossfades or ticks subtly.
